Ethiopia’s UNESCO World Heritage Masterpieces

Explore sacred sites, royal fortresses, and ancient kingdoms that tell the story of one of the world’s oldest continuous civilizations.

Rock-Hewn Churches of Lalibela

4–7 Days

Eleven monolithic churches carved entirely from solid volcanic rock in the 12th–13th centuries, often called the “New Jerusalem”.

  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Bete Giyorgis (St. George) – iconic cross-shaped church
  • Active pilgrimage site & ancient frescoes
  • Guided tours with expert historians

Fasil Ghebbi Royal Enclosure, Gondar

3–5 Days

A fortified royal city with castles, palaces, and churches built in the 17th century, known as the “Camelot of Africa”.

  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Castles of Fasilides, Iyasu & Mentewab
  • Bath of Fasilides & royal stables
  • Blend of Portuguese, Moorish & Ethiopian styles

Axum – Cradle of the Aksumite Empire

3–5 Days

Home to giant obelisks, ancient tombs, and ruins of one of Africa’s greatest civilizations (100–940 AD).

  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Stelae Park with 24-meter obelisk
  • Queen of Sheba’s Bath & ancient coins
  • Church of Our Lady Mary of Zion (Ark of the Covenant legend)
